Activities · Calistoga Area
Spa · Iconic

Indian Springs Resort

1712 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · Mud baths & mineral pools

The most iconic Calistoga spa — volcanic mud baths and a geothermal mineral pool. The pool is family-accessible and open to day guests. Mud bath treatments are adult-only. A genuinely memorable experience and one of the best flex afternoon options.

Book spa treatments in advance · Pool open for day use
Spa · Luxury

Solage Spa

755 Silverado Trail N · Calistoga · $$$$

Auberge's upscale spa with geothermal pools, a mud bar, and a full treatment menu. More polished than Indian Springs. The pools and setting are among the nicest in the valley — a great Thursday afternoon option before Friday's Napa day.

Family · Andrea-Friendly

Old Faithful Geyser

1299 Tubbs Ln · Calistoga · ~$20/person

One of only three "Old Faithful" geysers in the world, erupting every 20–30 minutes. Farm animals on site including goats and llamas — a big win with Andrea. No reservations needed, easy walk, genuinely fun for the whole group.

One of the easiest, most rewarding stops with a young child
Nature · Scenic

Palisades Trail

Calistoga · Moderate hike · Great views

A scenic hike above Calistoga with panoramic valley views. The lower section is accessible and rewarding without a full climb. Good for a Tuesday or Thursday morning before a later winery stop.

Best in the morning before the heat builds in June
Nature · Stroller-Friendly

Bale Grist Mill State Historic Park

~10 min south · Between Calistoga and St. Helena

A beautifully preserved 19th-century grist mill with a short, flat walking trail through a redwood grove. Completely stroller-friendly, genuinely pretty, and requires zero planning. Perfect for a casual morning with Andrea.

Adjacent to Bothe-Napa Valley State Park for longer hiking
Cycling

Calistoga Bikeshop

1318 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · Rentals & guided tours

Rent bikes and explore the vineyards at your own pace, or join a guided tour with winery stops built in. June weather is ideal for cycling. A great Tuesday or Thursday activity for the adults.

Winery · Experience

Castello di Amorosa

4045 N St Helena Hwy · Calistoga · Tickets required

A hand-built medieval Tuscan castle that's genuinely impressive. Family-friendly tours, great for photos, and solid wine. More of an experience than a standard tasting. Worth a half-morning if the group is up for it.

Book tickets ahead — fills quickly in June
Winery · Aerial Views

Sterling Vineyards

1111 Dunaweal Ln · Calistoga · Gondola ride up

Perched 300 feet above Calistoga, reached by a scenic gondola. Panoramic valley views and a self-guided tour make this one of the more memorable winery experiences nearby. The gondola is a hit with kids too.

Unique experience — book ahead in June
Winery · Intimate

Davis Estates

4060 Silverado Trail · Calistoga · By appointment · $$$

A stunning Howard Backen-designed estate that's been quietly turning heads since 2011. Cave tastings, coveted Phase V wines, and an intimate experience that rewards a booking. One of the area's hidden gems.

VIP cave experience particularly well reviewed
Air · Scenic

Hot Air Ballooning

Various operators · Calistoga / Yountville · Book in advance

The Napa Valley balloon experience is iconic — most operators launch from the Calistoga or Yountville area at sunrise, followed by a champagne breakfast. June weather is ideal. Book early as peak season slots go fast.

Napa Valley Balloons and Balloons Above the Valley are well-regarded
Walkable · Calistoga Main Street
Town Wander

Lincoln Avenue Stroll

Downtown Calistoga · All ages · No planning required

Calistoga's main drag is relaxed and walkable — boutique shops, tasting rooms, the Ca'toga Galleria d'Arte (Italian artist Carlo Marchiori's gallery, worth a pop-in), and the kind of laid-back small-town energy that makes it easy to fill a slow morning without an agenda.

Howell Mountain · Working down from O'Shaughnessy · Reserve ahead

Jeff's second Howell Mountain pick, visited on the way down from the peak. Same wide range — Zinfandel to Syrah — with boutique, high-elevation intensity that valley floor wines can't match. This is where the picnic most naturally lands if O'Shaughnessy didn't work out for it. Pack lunch from Cal Mart or grab something from the Inn's breakfast spread to bring along.

Afternoon · ~2:30 PM · Optional Drop-In

Cade Estate Winery

Howell Mountain · PlumpJack group · If the group has energy

The optional third stop — only if everyone's still feeling it. Cade is part of the PlumpJack family (already a 3-list Priority Pick), which makes it an interesting companion stop. Covers the full range from entry-level to high-end. A natural end to the mountain route before heading back down to Calistoga.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · PlumpJack family · Drop-in if energy allows — no pressure

Jeff's mountain day rules: Start at the highest point and work down — that's O'Shaughnessy first, always. Pack a picnic lunch (Cal Mart on Lincoln Ave is the easiest stop the morning of). Call all three wineries ahead of time — ask about bringing Andrea and whether you can eat on the property. Most will say yes. Coming off the mountain, you'll drop into St. Helena naturally — Charter Oak, Violetto, Charlie's, and Press are all right there. Or push back to Calistoga for an easy walk to Lovina.

Friday · June 19

Napa Day

Check out of The Inn on Pine by 11am Friday morning and head south. First stop is Robert Mondavi in Oakville — right on Hwy 29 on the way down. From there, continue into Napa city for a relaxed Oxbow lunch, evening dinner, and one night in a downtown hotel before the Sacramento drive Saturday.

1
Mid-Morning · ~11:30 AM–Noon

Robert Mondavi Winery

7801 St Helena Hwy, Oakville · On Hwy 29 en route to Napa

Check out of the Inn is at 11am, so plan to be on the road by 11:15. Mondavi is ~25 min south in Oakville — arriving around 11:30–noon works well for a mid-morning tasting. The most iconic winery in Napa Valley just reopened after two years — new state-of-the-art tasting facility, and Uncle Jeff says no one he knows has been yet. The winery owns To-Kalon vineyard, home to what are widely considered the most prized Cabernet grapes in North America. Reserve ahead.

Rec: Uncle Jeff (special mention) · Just reopened · Reserve ahead · Check Andrea policy · Allow ~25 min drive from Inn on Pine

Evening · 6:30–7 PM

Dinner in Napa

Choose one · All in or near downtown Napa

Three options ranging from lively bistro to upscale:


Angèle — Riverside French bistro, warm atmosphere, beautiful patio on the Napa River. A great send-off dinner for a group. Good options for Britt.

Morimoto Napa — Iron Chef Morimoto's Napa outpost. Upscale Japanese with excellent omakase and sashimi. Festive, visually impressive, naturally Britt-friendly. Great for a celebratory last night.

Compline — Wine bar, restaurant, and shop run by two sommeliers (one a master somm). On 1st St, walkable from the hotel. A global wine list that goes well beyond Napa, halibut on the dinner menu for Britt, and a great burger too. Eater SF top 14 — and the most wine-trip-appropriate dinner of the three.

Reserve as soon as the date is set — all three fill fast on summer Fridays. Compline: OpenTable · Angèle + Morimoto: book direct.

Hotel in Downtown Napa

One night · Check out Saturday morning

Archer Hotel Napa — Stylish boutique hotel right on First Street. Great location for walking to dinner and the market. Comfortable rooms and a solid rooftop.

Napa River Inn — Charming historic property directly on the Napa River, walkable to everything. Slightly more romantic feel than the larger hotels.

PlumpJack Winery

620 Oakville Cross Rd · By appointment · $$

Boutique Oakville estate with a rich Cabernet and an intimate, appointment-based experience. Jeff flagged PlumpJack's newest facility on Hwy 29 (next to Del Dada) as particularly worth visiting — great for a tour. Three independent recommendations make this the top unvisited priority.

Rec: Whitley Jacoby · Aunt Reg · Uncle Jeff · "Newest facility, great tour" — Jeff
🏆 Priority · 2 Lists

AXR Winery

Napa Valley · By appointment · $$

Four French winemakers and a billionaire — AXR has the only redwood forest in Napa Valley, where you can sit and taste under 200-year-old trees. Aunt Reg called it a recent favorite; Uncle Jeff independently put it on his list. A genuinely unique setting that no other winery in the valley can offer.

Rec: Aunt Reg · Uncle Jeff · "Only redwood forest in Napa Valley" — Jeff
🏆 Priority · 2 Lists

Peju Province Winery

8466 St Helena Hwy, Rutherford · Walk-ins welcome · $$

Right next door to Turnbull — Jeff specifically flagged this as the ideal walk-in add-on after the Wednesday tasting. Spanish family winery, now run by two sisters, with outdoor fountains, a little castle, garden seating, and a wide range of approachable, affordable wines. Walk up, no reservation needed.

Rec: Whitley Jacoby · Uncle Jeff · "Walk right in — easy add-on after Turnbull" — Jeff

Mountain AVAs · Uncle Jeff's Howell Mountain Day
Jeff's #1 · Start Here

O'Shaughnessy Estate Winery

Howell Mountain · By appointment · $$$

Uncle Jeff's top pick and the designated start for the mountain day — perched at the top of Howell Mountain, the highest point on his recommended route. The approach is top-of-the-mountain-down, so this is the first stop. Wide range: Zinfandel to Syrah, with something for everyone in the group.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · "Post at O'Shaughnessy at the top" — Jeff's exact words
Howell Mountain

Outpost Wines

Howell Mountain · By appointment · $$$

Jeff's second Howell Mountain call — like O'Shaughnessy, the range runs from Zinfandel to Syrah with plenty to taste. A boutique, high-elevation producer that delivers intensity and complexity you don't get from valley floor wines. Stop two on the way down the mountain.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · Zinfandel to Syrah range · Mountain intensity
Howell Mountain · PlumpJack Group

Cade Estate Winery

Howell Mountain · By appointment · $$–$$$

Part of the PlumpJack group — same family as the PlumpJack Priority Pick below. Cade covers the full range from entry level to high-end. Interesting for a comparison tasting if you're also planning a PlumpJack stop on a floor day. Jeff specifically placed it on Howell Mountain.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · PlumpJack family · Entry-level to high-end range
Howell Mountain · High Elevation

Robert Craig Winery

Howell Mountain · By appointment · $$

The highest-elevation stop on Jeff's Howell Mountain route. Robert Craig makes more affordable wines by Napa standards while still delivering mountain-AVA intensity. A good option if the group wants to add a fourth stop without breaking the budget at every winery.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · "If you want to get to really high elevation" · More affordable

Jeff's mountain day strategy: Drive to the highest peak first and work your way down. Start at O'Shaughnessy, then Outpost or Cade on the way down. Plan for ~3 wineries in a day. From Calistoga, Howell Mountain is a quick drive east — easy in and out. Call ahead about bringing Andrea, and ask if you can picnic on the property (most will say yes — pack lunch from Cal Mart or a local grocery and bring a cooler bag).

Calistoga AVA · Uncle Jeff's Local Picks
Calistoga · Jeff's #1

Venge Vineyards

Calistoga · Right in town · By appointment · $$

Uncle Jeff's top Calistoga pick — his words: "the best winery in Calistoga." Right in town on your doorstep. Fantastic wines and a cool intimate setting. The easiest possible flex day winery visit since there's no driving involved.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · "Right there, can't miss it" · Closest quality winery to base
Calistoga Area

Ravana Wines

Near Calistoga · By appointment · $$$

Founded by an Indian cardiologist who made his fortune in medical devices and turned it into serious wine. Jeff recommends it as a strong Calistoga-area option — not far from base. The backstory is interesting and the wines are well-regarded.

Rec: Uncle Jeff · Unique founder story · Worth a look for a flex day
Wildcard · Newly Reopened
Just Reopened · Rare Opportunity

Robert Mondavi Winery

7801 St Helena Hwy, Oakville · Reserve ahead · $$$

The most iconic winery in Napa Valley just reopened after being closed for two years following a family dispute. Jeff says the new facility is supposedly state-of-the-art and spectacular — and no one he knows has been yet. You'd be among the first. The winery owns To-Kalon vineyard (T-O-K-A-L-O-N), home to what are widely considered the most expensive and prized Cabernet grapes in North America — you'll see "To-Kalon" on labels throughout Napa, but only Mondavi has the vineyard itself. Reopened roughly three months ago, so the experience is still fresh and uncrowded. Jeff couldn't vouch for it personally, but flagged it as a rare and potentially special moment to visit.

Rec: Uncle Jeff (special mention) · "Just reopened · no one I know has been · To-Kalon vineyard" — Jeff
