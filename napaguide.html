Home Base · Mon–Thu

Calistoga & Larkmead

Home base is expected to be Calistoga or the Larkmead area just south on Hwy 29 — two of the most characterful parts of the valley. St. Helena is only 15 min south, making Wednesday's winery day seamless. Everything below is within 30 min of this base.

Larkmead vs. Calistoga: Larkmead is the stretch of Hwy 29 between Calistoga and St. Helena — more rural and vineyard-immersed, with Calistoga's restaurants and spas a short drive north and St. Helena's dining scene equally close to the south. Either works as a base with no meaningful difference in access.

Restaurants · Calistoga
Fine Dining · Michelin-Starred

Auro at Four Seasons

100 Silverado Trail N · Calistoga · $$$$

Calistoga's only Michelin-starred restaurant — refined seasonal tasting menus with vineyard and Mayacamas Mountain views. The wine program has received Wine Spectator's Best of Award of Excellence every year since 2023. Worth noting the chef who earned the star departed in early 2026, so check current reviews before booking.

Best for a special occasion dinner · Reserve well in advance
Dinner · Casual Elegant

Solbar at Solage

755 Silverado Trail N · Calistoga · $$$

The Solage resort's restaurant serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner with a seasonal farm-to-table focus. A bar menu of sushi and small bites makes it flexible for lighter evenings. One of the most consistently praised dining spots in Calistoga.

Great for any meal of the day · Bar menu good for casual evenings
Dinner · Downtown

Lovina

1457 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · $$$

A beloved downtown Calistoga spot run by chefs with San Francisco and wine country credentials. Eclectic American fare, consistently praised by locals and visitors. Weekend brunch also gets strong reviews.

Weekend brunch also excellent · Reservations recommended
Dinner · Cal-Italian

Bricco Osteria

1327 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · $$$

Cal-Italian farm-to-table in a downtown storefront. Known for excellent pasta — the seafood linguine is a standout. A warm, unfussy neighborhood feel that pairs well with a casual wine country evening.

Great for a mid-week dinner without needing a big occasion
Lunch · Casual

Cal Mart (Calistoga Market)

1491 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · $

One of Napa Valley's best deli counters — fresh sandwiches, local produce, and picnic supplies. Ideal for grabbing lunch before a winery visit or putting together a vineyard picnic. No fuss, no reservations, consistently excellent.

Perfect for a picnic lunch before hitting wineries
Breakfast · Coffee

Calistoga Roastery

1426 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · $

The go-to morning coffee stop in Calistoga. Committed to fresh-roasted beans, relaxed atmosphere, and a great way to start a slow morning before deciding what to do with the day.

The natural first stop on any Calistoga morning

Activities · Calistoga Area
Spa · Iconic

Indian Springs Resort

1712 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · Mud baths & mineral pools

The most iconic Calistoga spa — volcanic mud baths and a geothermal mineral pool. The pool is family-accessible and open to day guests. Mud bath treatments are adult-only. A genuinely memorable experience and one of the best flex afternoon options.

Book spa treatments in advance · Pool open for day use
Spa · Luxury

Solage Spa

755 Silverado Trail N · Calistoga · $$$$

Auberge's upscale spa with geothermal pools, a mud bar, and a full treatment menu. More polished than Indian Springs. The pools and setting are among the nicest in the valley — a great Thursday afternoon option before Friday's Napa day.

Family · Andrea-Friendly

Old Faithful Geyser

1299 Tubbs Ln · Calistoga · ~$20/person

One of only three "Old Faithful" geysers in the world, erupting every 20–30 minutes. Farm animals on site including goats and llamas — a big win with Andrea. No reservations needed, easy walk, genuinely fun for the whole group.

One of the easiest, most rewarding stops with a young child
Nature · Scenic

Palisades Trail

Calistoga · Moderate hike · Great views

A scenic hike above Calistoga with panoramic valley views. The lower section is accessible and rewarding without a full climb. Good for a Tuesday or Thursday morning before a later winery stop.

Best in the morning before the heat builds in June
Nature · Stroller-Friendly

Bale Grist Mill State Historic Park

~10 min south · Between Calistoga and St. Helena

A beautifully preserved 19th-century grist mill with a short, flat walking trail through a redwood grove. Completely stroller-friendly, genuinely pretty, and requires zero planning. Perfect for a casual morning with Andrea.

Adjacent to Bothe-Napa Valley State Park for longer hiking
Cycling

Calistoga Bikeshop

1318 Lincoln Ave · Calistoga · Rentals & guided tours

Rent bikes and explore the vineyards at your own pace, or join a guided tour with winery stops built in. June weather is ideal for cycling. A great Tuesday or Thursday activity for the adults.

Winery · Experience

Castello di Amorosa

4045 N St Helena Hwy · Calistoga · Tickets required

A hand-built medieval Tuscan castle that's genuinely impressive. Family-friendly tours, great for photos, and solid wine. More of an experience than a standard tasting. Worth a half-morning if the group is up for it.

Book tickets ahead — fills quickly in June
Winery · Aerial Views

Sterling Vineyards

1111 Dunaweal Ln · Calistoga · Gondola ride up

Perched 300 feet above Calistoga, reached by a scenic gondola. Panoramic valley views and a self-guided tour make this one of the more memorable winery experiences nearby. The gondola is a hit with kids too.

Unique experience — book ahead in June
Winery · Intimate

Davis Estates

4060 Silverado Trail · Calistoga · By appointment · $$$

A stunning Howard Backen-designed estate that's been quietly turning heads since 2011. Cave tastings, coveted Phase V wines, and an intimate experience that rewards a booking. One of the area's hidden gems.

VIP cave experience particularly well reviewed
Air · Scenic

Hot Air Ballooning

Various operators · Calistoga / Yountville · Book in advance

The Napa Valley balloon experience is iconic — most operators launch from the Calistoga or Yountville area at sunrise, followed by a champagne breakfast. June weather is ideal. Book early as peak season slots go fast.

Napa Valley Balloons and Balloons Above the Valley are well-regarded
Walkable · Calistoga Main Street
Town Wander

Lincoln Avenue Stroll

Downtown Calistoga · All ages · No planning required

Calistoga's main drag is relaxed and walkable — boutique shops, tasting rooms, the Ca'toga Galleria d'Arte (Italian artist Carlo Marchiori's gallery, worth a pop-in), and the kind of laid-back small-town energy that makes it easy to fill a slow morning without an agenda.

3601 St Helena Hwy N · ~10 min south of Calistoga

The Silverado Trail runs parallel to Hwy 29 along the eastern side of the valley. Slower, less commercial, and genuinely beautiful in June. Starts right at the edge of Calistoga — a great way to spend an afternoon even without a specific destination.

Napa Day

Check out of the St. Helena Airbnb Friday morning and shift to Napa for the final full day. Oxbow Market for browsing and lunch, a nearby winery in the afternoon, nice dinner, then one night in a downtown Napa hotel before the Sacramento drive Saturday.

1
Late Morning · ~11 AM

Oxbow Public Market

644 First St, Napa · Indoor market · Free entry

Napa's beloved indoor food market on the river. An excellent mix of artisan vendors — charcuterie, oysters, tacos, cheesemakers, bakeries, and the iconic Kara's Cupcakes. Great for browsing and easy grazing with Andrea. Grab lunch from multiple stalls and eat at the communal tables. Very relaxed, no reservations needed.

Hog Island Oyster Co. is right next door if the group wants a proper oyster lunch instead.
2
Early Afternoon · ~1:30–2 PM

Afternoon Winery

Choose one · All within 20 min of Oxbow

Three strong options depending on your group's preference:


Domaine Carneros — Carneros district, ~10 min from downtown Napa. A French-style château known for sparkling wines. The terrace tasting is one of the most scenic in the valley. Reserve ahead.

Trefethen Family Vineyards — Oak Knoll district, ~10 min. Historic wooden winery, excellent Chardonnay and Cabernet, more intimate feel. One of Napa's hidden gems.

Stag's Leap Wine Cellars — Silverado Trail, ~15 min. The winery that famously won the 1976 Paris Tasting. Historic significance + excellent Cabernet. Reservations required.

3
Evening · 6:30–7 PM

Dinner in Napa

Choose one · All in downtown or near Napa

Three options ranging from lively bistro to upscale:


Angèle — Riverside French bistro, warm atmosphere, great for a group, beautiful patio on the Napa River. A strong choice for a send-off dinner.

Morimoto Napa — Iron Chef Morimoto's Napa outpost. Upscale Japanese with excellent omakase and sashimi. Festive and visually impressive interior. Great for a celebratory last night.

The Thomas — Rooftop restaurant and bar in a historic building downtown. American cuisine, good cocktails, and a great view of the Napa River area. More casual than Morimoto but still a solid dinner.

Reserve Friday dinner as soon as the date is set — all three are popular on summer Fridays.
4
Night

Hotel in Downtown Napa

One night · Check out Saturday morning

Three good options for location and comfort:


Andaz Napa — Hyatt's boutique brand, central location, nice rooms, rooftop bar. Great value for the quality in Napa terms.

Archer Hotel Napa — Stylish boutique hotel right on First Street. Great location for walking to dinner and the market. Comfortable rooms and a solid rooftop.

Napa River Inn — Charming historic property directly on the Napa River, walkable to everything. Slightly more romantic feel than the larger hotels.
